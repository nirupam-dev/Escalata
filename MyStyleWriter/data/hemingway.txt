The old man sat alone at the bar. He drank his whiskey. The glass was empty. He did not ask for more. The bartender watched him. The old man said nothing. He put money on the counter. He left.

The sun came up over the hills. The river was cold. The trout moved in the current. Nick watched them. He did not cast his line yet. He waited. The morning was good and the water was clear.

The rain fell on the tin roof. She sat by the window. The cat was outside in the rain. She wanted to get the cat. The rain kept falling. She went out. The cat was gone. She came back wet.

He walked through the streets of Paris. The cafes were full. People talked and laughed. He did not stop. He kept walking. The river was ahead. He crossed the bridge. The city was behind him now.

The bull charged across the ring. The crowd shouted. The matador stood still. He did not move. The bull came close. The matador turned. The cape swung. The bull passed. The crowd was silent.

They sat at the table and did not talk. The wine was good. The bread was fresh. Outside the snow fell. They ate slowly. They did not need to speak. They understood each other.

The fish pulled the line tight. The old man held on. His hands were cut. The blood ran into the salt water. He did not let go. The fish was strong. The old man was stronger. He waited.

The train stopped at the station. The woman got off. She carried a small bag. The platform was empty. No one met her. She walked into the town alone. The train left.

He cleaned his rifle in the tent. The war was close. He could hear the guns. The other men slept. He could not sleep. He cleaned the rifle again. It was already clean. He did it anyway.

The boat moved through the gulf stream. The water was dark blue. The sun was hot on his back. He watched for fish. The sea was empty. He kept watching. A bird flew low over the water.

She cut her hair short. She looked at herself in the mirror. She did not smile. She looked different. She looked like someone she did not know. She put the scissors down. She left the room.

He sat in the cafe and wrote. The words came slowly. He crossed out a line. He wrote it again. Better this time. The coffee was cold. He did not notice. He kept writing.

The fight was over quickly. The big man went down. He did not get up. The crowd was quiet. Then they cheered. The winner stood alone in the ring. His hands hurt. He did not show it.

They drove through the countryside. The road was straight. The fields were green. She sat beside him. Neither spoke. The car moved fast. The trees went by. The sky was clear and blue.

The waiter brought more wine. The American did not want it. He drank it anyway. The cafe was closing. The chairs were stacked. He was the last one. He stood up. He paid. He left.

Nick set up camp by the river. He drove the stakes into the ground. The tent was small. He built a fire. He cooked beans. They were good. The fire cracked. The river made sounds in the dark.

The hospital was white and clean. The nurse came in. She did not smile. The soldier lay in the bed. His leg was gone. He looked at the ceiling. The nurse changed his bandage. She left.

He ordered two glasses of beer. The girl sat across from him. The hills were white in the sun. They waited for the train. It was hot. They drank the beer. It was cold and good.

The boxer trained every morning. He ran five miles. He hit the bag. He skipped rope. He was fast. He was not young anymore. But he was still fast. The fight was in two weeks.

The garden was dry. The sun had been strong all summer. The flowers were dead. She watered them anyway. She knew they would not come back. She watered them because there was nothing else to do.

He fished all day and caught nothing. The sun went down. The air grew cold. He packed his gear. He walked back to the car. The road was dark. He was not disappointed. He would come back tomorrow.

The hotel room was small. The bed was hard. The window looked out on the street. He could hear music from somewhere. He lay on the bed. He stared at the ceiling. He thought about nothing.

They crossed the river at dawn. The water was waist deep. The current was strong. They held their rifles above their heads. They made it across. Their boots were full of water. They kept going.

The market was loud and full of color. The fish lay on ice. The fruit was stacked high. He bought oranges. He paid too much. He did not care. The oranges were good.

She painted every afternoon. The light came through the north window. She painted the same view. The rooftops and the sky. Each time it was different. The light changed everything.

The dog lay in the shade. It was too hot to move. The street was empty. A car passed slowly. The dog watched it. Then it put its head back down. It slept.

He climbed the hill slowly. His knees hurt. The grass was tall. At the top he could see the valley. The river wound through it like a snake. He sat down. He rested. The view was worth the climb.

The whiskey burned his throat. He poured another. The room was dark. The radio played something soft. He sat in the chair. The bottle was almost empty. He did not care. Tonight he would drink.

They walked along the beach at sunset. The water was warm on their feet. The sand was orange in the light. They did not hold hands. They walked side by side. That was enough.

The plane came in low over the trees. The engine sputtered. He held the stick steady. The runway was short. He brought it down hard. The wheels hit dirt. He stopped. He was alive.

The kitchen was warm. The bread was baking. She wiped her hands on her apron. The children were outside. She could hear them shouting. She smiled. The bread smelled good. The house was full of it.

He read the letter twice. Then he folded it. He put it in his pocket. He did not say what was in it. She asked. He shook his head. Some things you do not say.

The wind blew hard off the lake. The waves were white. The boat rocked. He held the tiller with both hands. The sail was full. They moved fast. The shore grew smaller behind them.

She danced alone in the empty room. There was no music. She did not need it. She moved slowly. Her shadow moved on the wall. She was graceful. She was sad. She kept dancing.

The road ended at the sea. He stopped the car. He got out. The waves broke on the rocks below. The spray came up cold. He stood at the edge. He breathed the salt air. He turned back.

They ate dinner in silence. The food was simple. Bread and cheese and wine. The candle flickered. Their shadows moved on the walls. When they finished she cleared the table. He watched her move.

The morning was cold. Frost covered the grass. The horse stamped its feet. He saddled it quickly. His fingers were stiff. He rode out across the field. The air was sharp and clean.

He fixed the fence in the rain. The posts were rotten. The wire was rusty. He pulled the old wire off. He stretched new wire. His hands were cut. The rain kept coming. He kept working.

The bar was crowded. Smoke hung in the air. The music was too loud. He found a spot at the end. He ordered bourbon. He drank it slowly. He watched the people. He knew none of them.

She wrote the letter carefully. She chose each word. She did not say what she felt. She said other things. Things that were safe. She sealed the envelope. She did not send it.

The lake was still at dawn. The mist hung low over the water. The canoe moved without sound. He paddled slowly. A loon called from somewhere. The sound carried across the water. It was lonely and beautiful.

The ring of the telephone woke him. He did not answer it. It rang again. He lay in the dark. It stopped. He closed his eyes. It rang again. He picked it up. He said nothing.

The bullfight started at five. The stands were full. The sun was still high. The first bull was small and fast. The matador was young. He was afraid. He did not show it. The crowd did not know.

They sat on the porch and watched the storm come. The clouds were dark. The wind picked up. Lightning flashed over the hills. The thunder came after. They did not go inside. They watched.

He sharpened the axe on the stone. The blade was dull from use. He worked it slowly. Back and forth. The metal sang against the stone. When it was sharp he split wood. The logs cracked clean.

The café was nearly empty. A man sat in the corner reading a newspaper. The waiter stood behind the bar. It was late. The man ordered another coffee. The waiter brought it. Neither spoke.

She ran along the river path. Her breathing was steady. The trees were bare. Leaves covered the ground. Her feet crunched through them. She ran faster. The cold air filled her lungs. She felt alive.

The hunting was bad that year. The deer had moved to higher ground. They tracked them for three days. The snow was deep. They found nothing. On the fourth day they went home. They did not speak of it.

He sat on the dock and watched the boats come in. The fishermen unloaded their catch. The gulls circled and screamed. The fish shone silver in the bins. The smell was strong. He liked it.

The room was full of books. He had read most of them. Some twice. The ones he loved were worn. The spines were cracked. The pages were soft. He picked one up. He read the first page. He read it again.

She swam far out from the shore. The water was deep and dark beneath her. She floated on her back. The sky was wide and empty. She was alone. The shore was distant. She was not afraid.

The train moved through the night. The lights of towns passed by. He pressed his face to the glass. The towns were small and dark. People slept in them. He could not sleep. The train kept moving.

The vineyard stretched across the hillside. The grapes were heavy on the vines. The sun was warm. The workers moved between the rows. They picked quickly. Their baskets filled. The harvest was good this year.

He walked into the ring. The lights were bright. The crowd was noise. His opponent was bigger. That did not matter. Speed mattered. Timing mattered. He touched his gloves together. The bell rang.

The cemetery was quiet. The stones were old. Some names were worn away. He found the one he was looking for. He stood before it. He did not cry. He had done that already. He just stood there.

She opened the shop at seven. The street was still dark. She turned on the lights. She made coffee. She arranged the flowers in their buckets. She unlocked the door. No one came for an hour. She waited.

The ice was thick on the pond. The boys played hockey. Their shouts carried across the cold air. Their sticks cracked against the ice. The puck slid fast. Their breath made clouds. They played until dark.

He drove all night. The highway was empty. The headlights cut through the dark. The radio played static. He turned it off. There was only the engine and the road. Mile after mile. He did not stop.

The mountain was there every morning when she woke. It did not change. The snow on top. The trees below. The rock face in between. She looked at it every morning. It was always the same. It was always different.

They met at the station. She was early. He was late. She saw him coming through the crowd. She did not wave. He found her. They looked at each other. He picked up her bag. They walked out together.

The storm passed in the night. The morning was clear. Branches lay across the yard. The creek was swollen and brown. He walked outside. The air smelled clean. Washed clean. Everything was bright and wet.

The guitar was old. The wood was dark. He played it every night. The same songs. Songs his father played. The strings buzzed on the frets. He did not mind. The sound was enough. It filled the room.

She stood at the window watching the rain. The street was empty. The gutters ran with water. A taxi passed slowly. Its lights reflected on the wet pavement. She turned away from the window. She made tea.

The desert stretched in every direction. The sand was white. The heat came in waves. He walked slowly. His water was low. The town was still far. He kept walking. There was nothing else to do.

They fished from the bridge. The water below was deep and green. The current moved slowly. A turtle surfaced and went back down. They dropped their lines. They waited. The sun was warm on their backs.

He closed the book. He put it on the shelf. He turned off the lamp. The room was dark. He lay in bed. He listened to the sounds outside. A dog barking. A car passing. Then silence. He slept.

The harbor was full of boats. The masts rocked gently. The water slapped against the hulls. He walked along the dock. The wood was weathered and gray. He found his boat. It was small but good. He got in.

She cooked the meal from memory. No recipe. Just her hands and the food. The onions sizzled. The garlic filled the air. She stirred the pot. She tasted it. More salt. She stirred again. It was right.

The valley was green after the rain. The creek ran clear over the stones. The cattle stood in the meadow. They chewed and looked at nothing. The farmer leaned on the fence. He watched them. He was content.

She stood at the edge of the diving board. The pool was blue below. The water was still. She bounced once. Twice. She dove. Clean entry. No splash. She came up. She swam to the edge. She climbed out.

The letter arrived on Monday. He knew the handwriting. He did not open it right away. He made coffee first. He sat down. He opened it. He read it slowly. Then he read it again. He folded it. He put it away.

They climbed the tower. The stairs were narrow and stone. Round and round. Higher and higher. At the top the wind was strong. They could see the whole city. The river. The bridges. The roofs. The sky.
